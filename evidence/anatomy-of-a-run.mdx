A ShoppiPanel study is a population of _trips_. Each trip is one AI shopping agent, asked your category's question once, doing what it actually does: reaching the web, reading or being cited, deciding, and answering. The report you read is a projection of those trips.

The promise is narrow and testable: **no claim in the report floats.** Every recommendation, every count, every "this is what the agents did" carries at least one reference to a specific piece of captured evidence, and that evidence is retrievable. Every number walks back to exactly the trips that produced it.

## What one trip records

When a trip runs, three things about it are written down as they happen, not reconstructed afterward:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="The transcript">
    The full turn-by-turn record of what the agent was asked, what it reasoned, and what it said. The agent's own words, captured at the time, with credential and secret-token patterns scrubbed.
  </Card>

  <Card title="The journey">
    Which sources the trip actually touched: the pages a browsing agent fetched, or the sources a search-native agent (Perplexity Sonar) was given as citations. Each source is recorded with where it came from.
  </Card>

  <Card title="The access posture">
    For a browsing agent, whether it _reached_ a page and read it, or was _access-limited_ on a page that turns away automated traffic. Recorded per attempt, as a graded outcome, never collapsed to a single absolute word.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

A trip also carries the plain facts you would expect: which model family stood in for it (GPT, Gemini, Claude, or Perplexity Sonar), which lane it ran (browse or search-native), and whether it completed with enough evidence to count. Those travel with the trip as a stamped record: a trip is an addressable unit with a provenance.

## From a source the agent touched to a receipt you can open

Every source a trip used becomes one **source card**: a small, uniform record that says where the source came from (a page the agent fetched, or a source the search-native model cited), what was on it, and which trip used it. The two lanes produce the same kind of card, so a browse trip and a search-native trip both resolve to the same evidence currency and fold into one denominator. ([Two Lanes](/how-it-works/two-lanes) covers how the two retrieval modes are captured and why they reconcile to one currency.)

In a shipped report package, the evidence ledger is not a summary. It is the cards themselves. Each card names the trip it came from, the source it represents, and its locator (for a browse source, the URL the agent actually opened). It also marks honestly what kind of claim it can support: a card that records _what an agent did_ is kept distinct from a card that asserts _a fact about a product_. A count claim and a product-fact claim are never backed by the same undifferentiated blob.

When the report states a number, the cards behind it are retrievable, and each card points at the trip and the source it came from.

## Counts before rates

Before the report states any rate or any verdict, it states the counts. A shipped report carries a count ledger that declares, for the study, how many trips were planned, completed, did-not-run, insufficient, and metric-eligible, so the denominator is a first-class fact you can check the rate against. [Reading the Count Ledger](/insights/reading-a-report/count-ledger) teaches how each line is defined and read.

Every line in that ledger carries its own evidence references back to the trips it summarizes. And the winner a trip reports is the winner the agent itself declared, carried through to the count as a structured entity, not a word guessed back out of its prose afterward. That sourced count is what the report's headline is computed from before any sentence is written, a mechanism [The Honesty Layer](/evidence/honesty-layer) lays out in full.

## Access posture, stated honestly

A browsing trip regularly meets pages that don't serve automated traffic. Per attempt, the report records whether the trip _reached_ a page or was _access-limited_, and an access-limited trip is counted _as_ access-limited in the ledger rather than converted into a false negative about your brand. [Reach](/insights/reading-a-report/reach) defines the reached / access-limited posture and its attribution discipline in full.

## Retrievable, not just claimed

The report's evidence (its readable report and its receipts appendix) is produced as part of the report package and is retrievable through an **owner-scoped read path**. That path only just landed, so during the pilot we open it for you on request, and any number in a report can be audited back to its trips and sources. Retrieval requires no server access. The package itself carries a content hash and pointers to its core receipts, so a delivered report can be located and its evidence read.

Every served surface of the report passes through the same sanitization that keeps internal vocabulary off the buyer face: the served receipt is in the buyer's register, not the engine's.

To be precise about what is shipped versus what is being wired:

- **Rolling out:** the owner-scoped read path described above, which makes the evidence retrievable.
- **Being wired now:** the polished self-serve walk-back, clicking straight from a headline number, to the trip, to that trip's transcript and the sources it touched, without leaving the product.

## The chain a number rests on

A number rests on a chain, every link of which is recorded, addressable, and auditable.

## Short answers

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title='If a report says "19 of 25 agents surfaced my brand," can I see the 19?'>
    Yes. The count is backed by evidence references that resolve to the trips behind it, and each trip's evidence (what it touched, what it said) is in the report's evidence ledger. The number is sourced, not asserted.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What exactly do you capture per agent?">
    Its transcript (turn-by-turn, with secret-token patterns scrubbed), its journey (the sources it actually used and where they came from), its access posture (reached vs. access-limited), and the plain facts: model family (GPT, Gemini, Claude, or Perplexity Sonar), lane, and whether it counted.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens when an agent couldn't reach my page?">
    The report says _access-limited_ and counts that trip honestly in the denominator. A reach gap on one run is never converted into a false fact about your site or a fake trend.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can a finding exist without evidence behind it?">
    No. Every recommendation, finding, and count carries at least one evidence reference; in a shipped report the evidence ledger is the cards themselves, not a summary of them.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I need engineering help to get the receipts?">
    No. The report's readable view and receipts appendix are retrievable through the owner-scoped read path, with no server access required. As described under [Retrievable, not just claimed](#retrievable-not-just-claimed), that path only just landed, so during the pilot we open it for you on request. The polished self-serve click-through is being wired.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
