You ask a category question. The standard panel runs it. The report lands on your desk: the read, the structured data, and a receipt for every number.

You operate the two ends of a study, the **question** you ask and the **report** you read, and the rig runs everything in between. It's the posture built for a team that wants a category read on a cadence, not a console to configure.

## What a Pro engagement is

A study runs in five stages: question, panel, runs, evidence, report. In Pro you touch the first and the last; the rig owns the middle.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="The Question You Ask" icon="circle-question">
    A category question (_"what's the best mid-size SUV,"_ _"how should I take payments online"_) plus the brands and surfaces that matter to you. It runs against the standard panel, in its set configuration.
  </Card>

  <Card title="The Report You Read" icon="file-chart-column">
    The report package lands the same way every time: a one-line read, the structured data behind it, and the evidence the numbers rest on. Five-minute read at the top, auditable line by line underneath.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

You are not configuring an instrument each time you run. The panel is fixed at the version your baseline was calibrated on, so two reads a quarter apart stay comparable. See [Panel as an instrument](/how-it-works/panel-as-an-instrument). Composing the panel, dispatching the live-web runs, capturing every trip, and binding the report to its evidence: that's the rig's job, not yours.

For the full anatomy of what comes back, see [What you get](/insights/overview).

You stay in the language of your category (your brands, your competitors, your question) and never operate the measurement layer underneath. If configuring that layer is the work you actually need, that's the [Research tier](/offered/research-tier), not Pro.

## What you get back

Every Pro study returns one artifact (the report package) in three bound layers, so the headline can never claim a result the counts don't support (the [honesty layer](/evidence/honesty-layer)).

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="The Read" icon="file-chart-column">
    A one-line verdict, the distribution behind it, and the strategic "so what." The five-minute read.
  </Card>

  <Card title="The Structured Data" icon="layers">
    Machine-readable records: which agent landed where, per-trip outcomes, the count ledger, and the access posture of every surface touched. The line-by-line record.
  </Card>

  <Card title="The Evidence" icon="receipt">
    Each agent's trip and the sources it touched: what it reached and what was **access-limited**. The receipts the numbers rest on, not a summary of them.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  Surface reach is reported per finding as **reached** or **access-limited**. See [Reach](/insights/reading-a-report/reach).
</Note>

## Cadences that fit Pro

Pro is a recurring read, not a one-off. Teams run it on a rhythm: same question, same panel version, comparable read to read.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Quarterly Baseline" icon="clock">
    The same mission every quarter, on the same panel version: a durable trendline of how agents see your category, comparable read to read.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Competitive Watch" icon="radar">
    A standing read on where you land against named rivals in the agent's answer, refreshed on a cadence you set.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Pre / Post Launch" icon="split">
    A controlled before-and-after on the same instrument: did a site change, a campaign, or a new page move how agents shop you?
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

All three lean on the same property: a fixed panel version makes successive reads comparable ([Panel as an instrument](/how-it-works/panel-as-an-instrument)), which turns a one-time snapshot into a trendline. The mechanics of each cadence are in [Cadences & use cases](/insights/cadences-and-use-cases).

## When Pro is the right posture

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Pick Pro When" icon="circle-check">
    You want a recurring category read on a fixed instrument, your team works in the language of brands and questions rather than panels and cohorts, and comparability across reads matters more than control over the rig.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Pick Research When" icon="wrench">
    You need to set the panel, the model mix, or the persona cohorts, design your own missions, or work the raw evidence layer directly. That's [the Research tier](/offered/research-tier).
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Most engagements settle into Pro. The reason is in the lifecycle: an engagement opens with a managed baseline we calibrate to your category and an enablement phase that trains your team on that calibrated rig. As self-service dispatch rolls out, a team graduates into composing a study and reading its receipts on their own, and the standing need that remains is the recurring read, which is exactly what Pro is. Research stays available alongside it for the work that genuinely needs the rig opened up.

See [the engagement lifecycle](/offered/engagement-lifecycle) for how a team arrives at this posture.

<Note>
  Commercials are scoped per engagement: the panel size, the cadence, and the posture you start in all factor in. Pricing is set with you, not published here.
</Note>
