Research is the posture where you set the instrument, not just the question. You configure the panel, the model mix, the persona cohorts, and the missions — and you take the raw evidence layer to work the structured data directly, rather than reading the report we compute from it.

It is the same rig as [Pro](/offered/pro-tier), the same receipted evidence, the same honest report. The difference is reach: in Pro you bring the question and read the result; in Research you open the middle of the study and shape what runs.

## What you configure

A study runs in five stages — question, panel, runs, evidence, report. Pro hands you the first and the last. Research opens the middle.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="The Panel" icon="layer-group">
    The set of agents a mission runs against, and how a finding is denominated across them. Tuned to your category instead of left on the standard configuration.
  </Card>

  <Card title="The Model Mix" icon="split">
    Which agents are in the panel and at what weight — GPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity Sonar, and the browse-native versus search-native lanes each one runs. You set the composition your category cares about.
  </Card>

  <Card title="The Persona Cohorts" icon="users">
    The shopper profiles a mission runs as — a first-time buyer, a price-led comparison shopper, a returning customer with a brand in mind. You choose which cohorts the panel shops as, so the read reflects how your real buyers ask.
  </Card>

  <Card title="The Missions" icon="route">
    The category questions themselves — the exact prompts a real shopper would ask, scoped to the brands and surfaces you care about. You design them rather than picking from a standard set.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

A **persona cohort** is the shopper profile a mission runs as. One mission — _"what's the best mid-size SUV"_ — reads differently when an agent runs it as a first-time buyer than as someone returning with a make already in mind. Configuring the cohorts is how you make the panel ask the way your category's buyers actually ask.

## Taking the raw evidence layer

Both postures return the same three-layer package — the read, the structured data, and the receipts. Pro gives you the read plus its structured data. Research adds the layer underneath: the **raw evidence**, the queryable records every number is computed from.

Working that layer directly means you can:

- Re-aggregate the trips on your own cut — by cohort, by agent, by surface — instead of reading only the slices the report rolled up.
- Join the structured records against your own data in your own environment.
- Audit a finding down to the individual trip — the steps the agent took, the sources it touched, and whether each one was **reached** or **access-limited** — and build your own analysis on top.

The binding still holds: the read is computed from the data, the data from the evidence, so a headline can never assert a result the counts don't support. Research gives you the evidence in a form you can work, not a looser claim. The [Under the hood](/under-the-hood/overview) section is the mechanism behind that binding.

## When Research over Pro

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Your category needs a non-standard panel" icon="wrench">
    The agents, weights, or retrieval lanes that matter for how your buyers shop aren't the standard configuration. You tune the instrument to the category instead of accepting the default.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Your buyers don't ask one way" icon="users">
    The read changes by who's asking. You need the panel to shop as several distinct persona cohorts and to see each cohort's result separately.
  </Card>

  <Card title="You work the data, not just the report" icon="layers">
    Your team has its own analysts and its own questions of the evidence. You want the structured records to join, re-cut, and build on — not only the read we computed.
  </Card>

  <Card title="The missions are bespoke" icon="route">
    The questions worth asking in your category are specific enough that a standard mission set won't capture them. You design the missions yourself.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

If none of those hold — you want a recurring read on a fixed instrument and the report is the deliverable — [Pro](/offered/pro-tier) is the posture, and it's where most engagements land after enablement.

## Available from baseline

Research isn't a later add-on. It's the natural shape of the **baseline phase**, when configuring the instrument to your category _is_ the work.

<Note>
  During baseline, calibrating the panel to your category — tightening the agents, the cohorts, and the missions until the read is stable and the receipts hold — is exactly the Research posture, run hands-on with you. A panel that isn't calibrated to your category reads noise as signal, so this configuration is the engagement, not a formality.
</Note>

That's why the control posture and the early phase line up. The work of getting the instrument right is the same work as operating it with full control. From there, an engagement can settle into Pro for recurring reads, or stay in Research where the configuration keeps changing. See the [engagement lifecycle](/offered/engagement-lifecycle) for how the phases move.

<Note>
  Commercials are scoped per engagement — the panel you configure, the cohorts, the mission set, and the cadence all factor in. Pricing is set with you, not published here.
</Note>
