Access today is scoped to your engagement. Your team reaches your reports and the receipts behind them through a gated workspace; the data we deliver into your own environment is yours and stays there. SSO, roles, and org-wide admin tooling are on the roadmap — set per engagement until the shared model ships.

## What access looks like today

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Your Reports, Gated" icon="shield-check">
    Your team reaches your delivered reports — the read, the structured data, and the evidence cards behind every number — through a private delivery surface. Today that surface is reached by an unguessable private link, scoped to your engagement and shared only with the people named on it; it is not yet per-user, token-scoped authorization. That finer control — sign-in tied to each named user — arrives with the shared workspace (in the "coming" set below).
  </Card>

  <Card title="Your Data, In Your Environment" icon="receipt">
    Beyond in-product access, the report package is delivered into an environment you control. Once it lands there it is yours — to keep, to re-house, to build on. That copy does not depend on your workspace staying open.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

The boundary is one tenant per engagement. A study, its runs, its evidence, and its reports belong to your engagement and are reached only by the people named on it. We scope that boundary with you at the start, and it holds for the life of the engagement.

<Note>
  When an engagement ends, any data already delivered into your own environment stays yours — there is nothing you are required to delete. In-product workspace access ends; the specifics are set in enterprise contracting. The same boundary is described from the lifecycle side in [Engagement lifecycle](/offered/engagement-lifecycle).
</Note>

## Who reaches what

Both postures share the same access shape — what differs is how much of the rig a given person operates, not who can see your data.

| | Pro — graduates into self-serve | Research — configure the rig |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Reaches your reports** | Named users on your engagement | Named users on your engagement |
| **Dispatches studies** | Your team, after enablement | Your team, after enablement |
| **Configures the panel** | Configured for you | Your team, on the tuned instrument |
| **Receives the delivered package** | Into your environment | Into your environment, plus the raw evidence layer |

The two postures are detailed in [How it's offered](/offered/overview). Access is the same line across both: your engagement, your named users, your environment.

## By design — coming with the shared workspace

A single-sign-on integration, named roles with different permissions, and org-wide admin tooling are part of the multi-tenant workspace we are building, not capabilities that ship today. We will not describe them as live before they are. Where a procurement review needs one of these now, it is handled per engagement in contracting — not self-served from an admin console that does not yet exist.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Single Sign-On" icon="shield-check">
    **By design — coming.** SSO against your identity provider is on the roadmap for the shared workspace. Until it lands, sign-in is provisioned per named user on your engagement. If SSO is a procurement requirement for your pilot, raise it and we scope it with you.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Roles & Permissions" icon="users">
    **Rolling out — targeted Q3.** Distinct roles — who can dispatch a study versus who can only read the result — and per-user sign-in arrive with the shared workspace. Today, access is granted per named user; a finer-grained role split is scoped per engagement until then.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Org Admin Console" icon="window">
    **By design — coming.** A self-service admin surface — invite a teammate, set their access, see who reached what — is part of the multi-tenant build. Today, user provisioning for your engagement is handled with us directly rather than from a console.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Multi-Workspace / Portfolio" icon="layer-group">
    **By design — coming.** Scoping studies across a portfolio of brands or properties under one parent organization is roadmap work. Where a portfolio split matters for your pilot, we tag and scope it per engagement so the shared model is not a rewrite later.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  A capability is described as live only when it is live. Everything in this section is roadmap or scoped-per-engagement, and is marked that way. If a date or a guarantee matters to your review, it is set in contracting.
</Note>

## What to ask for now

If your procurement or security review needs something in the "coming" set before the shared workspace ships, it does not block the engagement — it gets scoped.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Name the requirement">
    Tell us which control your review requires — SSO against your IdP, a read-only versus dispatch split, a portfolio scoping boundary. Concrete beats general.
  </Step>
  <Step title="We scope it per engagement">
    We set how that control is handled for your pilot in contracting — provisioning, the access boundary, and what is delivered into your environment. The boundary is written down, not assumed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="It folds into the shared model later">
    When the multi-tenant workspace ships, the per-engagement scoping is built to fold into it rather than be replaced. Your pilot is not a throwaway path.
  </Step>
</Steps>
