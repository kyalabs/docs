An engagement is not a tool you switch on. It's a path: we calibrate the instrument to your category, train your team on the calibrated rig, then graduate you into running studies on demand. The same receipted evidence and the same honest report come back at every phase — what changes is who operates the rig. The baseline phase is live today; in-product self-service is rolling out.

The three phases run in order. Each one ends on a result you can see, not a date.

## Baseline — we calibrate the instrument to your category

A panel that isn't calibrated to your category reads noise as signal. So before any number is one you'd put in front of a board, we tune the instrument to your category and your brands until the read holds steady. This is the work, not a setup formality — and we do it with you, hands-on, rather than handing you a generic dial on day one.

In this phase we run a series of tuning studies **for** you. Each one tightens the panel and the missions against what came back: the question gets sharper, the persona cohorts get closer to your real buyers, the surfaces under test get pinned to the ones that matter. We read the receipts, adjust, and run again — until two studies of the same question return the same shape.

## Enablement — we train your team on the calibrated rig

Once the read is stable, the question shifts from _is the instrument right?_ to _can your team operate it?_ Enablement is the bridge. We train your team on the rig as it was calibrated — not on a generic demo account, on your panel.

Three things your team leaves knowing how to do:

- **Compose a study** — frame a category question, pick the panel version, set the missions and cohorts that make the read mean something.
- **Read a report and its receipts** — walk a headline number back to the trips and sources behind it, and recognize when a surface came back **reached** versus **access-limited**.
- **Dispatch a run and read what lands** — start a study in-product and receive the same report package the baseline produced.

## Self-service — you run studies on demand

This is the phase an engagement graduates into. You dispatch studies yourself, in-product, against the panel version your baseline was calibrated on — new reads landing on your desk the same way the baseline did, with the same evidence currency, the same honest report, the same lifecycle. Nothing about the deliverable changes; only who pressed start. In-product self-service is rolling out — until it lands, the same studies run managed, on your cadence.

Because the panel version is fixed to the one your baseline calibrated, the studies you run here are comparable to the studies we ran at baseline. A quarterly baseline, a competitive watch, a controlled before-and-after on a launch — all run on the same instrument, all comparable read to read. The [Pro tier — self-serve](/offered/pro-tier) is this posture: you bring the question and the cadence, the standard panel runs it, the report comes back.

## Access, admin, and continuity

Access today is scoped to your engagement. Your team reaches your reports and the receipts behind them through a gated delivery surface — an unguessable private link scoped to your engagement, shared with the people named on it. Per-user sign-in and SSO arrive with the shared workspace; until then, access is provisioned per named user.

What a procurement review asks about the end of an engagement:

- **Your data stays yours.** Any report package, structured data, or evidence already delivered into your own environment is yours to keep. There is nothing you are required to delete, and nothing that expires out from under you.
- **In-product access ends with the engagement.** The ability to dispatch new studies — in-product as that rolls out, managed until then — is tied to the active engagement. When it ends, that access ends; the work already in your hands does not.
- **The specifics live in enterprise contracting.** Retention windows, export formats, and the terms of wind-down are set in the contract, scoped to your engagement.

**SSO, named roles, an admin console, and portfolio scoping** are part of the shared multi-tenant workspace being built — role-based access is targeted for Q3 ([roadmap](/roadmap)). Until they ship, each is scoped per engagement in contracting rather than self-served. Name the control your review requires and we scope it for your pilot; it folds into the shared model later rather than being replaced.
