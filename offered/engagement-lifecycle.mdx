An engagement is not a tool you switch on. It's a path: we calibrate the instrument to your category, train your team on the calibrated rig, then hand you the keys to run studies on demand. The same receipted evidence and the same honest report come back at every phase — what changes is who operates the rig.

The three phases run in order. Each one ends on a result you can see, not a date.

## Baseline — we calibrate the instrument to your category

A panel that isn't calibrated to your category reads noise as signal. So before any number is one you'd put in front of a board, we tune the instrument to your category and your brands until the read holds steady. This is the work, not a setup formality — and we do it with you, hands-on, rather than handing you a generic dial on day one.

In this phase we run a series of tuning studies **for** you. Each one tightens the panel and the missions against what came back: the question gets sharper, the persona cohorts get closer to your real buyers, the surfaces under test get pinned to the ones that matter. We read the receipts, adjust, and run again — until two studies of the same question return the same shape.

The Research posture lives here. If configuring the instrument — the panel, the model mix, the persona cohorts, the missions — is itself the work you need, that's the control you take during baseline and can keep. See [Research tier — custom](/offered/research-tier).

## Enablement — we train your team on the calibrated rig

Once the read is stable, the question shifts from _is the instrument right?_ to _can your team operate it?_ Enablement is the bridge. We train your team on the rig as it was calibrated — not on a generic demo account, on your panel.

Three things your team leaves knowing how to do:

- **Compose a study** — frame a category question, pick the panel version, set the missions and cohorts that make the read mean something.
- **Read a report and its receipts** — walk a headline number back to the trips and sources behind it, and recognize when a surface came back **reached** versus **access-limited**.
- **Dispatch a run and read what lands** — start a study in-product and receive the same report package the baseline produced.

## Self-service — you run studies on demand

This is where most engagements live. You dispatch studies yourself, in-product, against the panel version your baseline was calibrated on. New reads land on your desk the same way the baseline did — same evidence currency, same honest report, same lifecycle. Nothing about the deliverable changes; only who pressed start.

Because the panel version is fixed to the one your baseline calibrated, the studies you run now are comparable to the studies we ran then. A quarterly baseline, a competitive watch, a controlled before-and-after on a launch — all run on the same instrument, all comparable read to read. The [Pro tier — self-serve](/offered/pro-tier) is this posture: you bring the question and the cadence, the standard panel runs it, the report comes back.

## Continuity of access

Two questions a procurement review always asks: _what happens to our data, and what happens to our access, when the engagement ends?_

- **Your data stays yours.** Any report package, structured data, or evidence already delivered into your own environment is yours to keep. There is nothing you are required to delete, and nothing that expires out from under you.
- **In-product access ends with the engagement.** The ability to dispatch new studies in-product is tied to the active engagement. When it ends, that access ends — but the work already in your hands does not.
- **The specifics live in enterprise contracting.** Retention windows, export formats, and the exact terms of wind-down are set in the contract, scoped to your engagement — not published here.

<Note>
  Workspace access, roles, and administrative controls are scoped per engagement and set up with you during enablement. See [Access and admin](/offered/access-and-admin) for how that's handled.
</Note>
