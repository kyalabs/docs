You don't buy a tool you have to learn before it works. You start with studies run **for** you, on a panel we calibrate to your category until the read is stable — then you graduate into running them **yourself**, on demand, on that same calibrated instrument (self-service is rolling out). The two postures below are the two ends of that path.

## Two postures

A study runs in five stages — question (①), panel (②), runs (③), evidence (④), report (⑤); the two postures differ in which of those you operate yourself.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Pro — self-serve" icon="play">
    **Ask a category question; the report lands on your desk.** You bring the question, the standard panel runs it, and you read the result plus its structured data. On-demand reads in your category, on a standard panel and a set configuration.

    You touch the **question (①)** and the **report (⑤)** — the rig runs the middle. Where most engagements land after enablement.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Custom — configure the rig" icon="wrench">
    **Configure the instrument and take the raw evidence layer.** You set the panel, the model mix, and the persona cohorts (the shopper profiles a mission runs as); you design the missions and work the structured data directly. Control beyond Pro.

    You open the **panel (②)**, the **runs (③)**, and the **evidence (④)** — and take the data. Available from the baseline phase, when configuring the instrument to your category is the work.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Both postures return the same receipted evidence and the same honest report — they differ only in how much of the rig you operate yourself. [What You Get](/what-you-get) covers the deliverable both return.

|  | Pro — self-serve | Custom — configure the rig |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **You configure** | The question and the cadence | The panel, model mix, persona cohorts, and missions |
| **You receive** | The read \+ structured data | The same, plus the raw evidence layer to work directly |
| **Panel** | Standard panel, set configuration | Tuned to your priorities |
| **Best when** | Recurring reads on a fixed instrument | You need control beyond the standard rig |

## The engagement lifecycle

An engagement moves through three phases — from a baseline we calibrate to your category, to studies you run yourself on demand:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Baseline — we calibrate the instrument to your category">
    We run a series of tuning studies **for** you, hands-on, tightening the panel and the missions to your category and your brands until the read is stable and the receipts hold. A panel that isn't calibrated to your category reads noise as signal — so the calibration is the work, not a formality, and we do it with you rather than handing you a generic dial on day one. Your team's load is the inputs and the sign-off, not the operation. You leave Baseline when the trendline starts from a stable zero — the panel reads your category consistently, run to run.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enablement — we train your team on the calibrated rig">
    Training on the calibrated rig: how studies are composed, how to read a report and its receipts, how to dispatch your own. You leave this phase able to run the instrument without us in the loop.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Self-service — you run studies on demand (rolling out)">
    The phase you graduate into: dispatching studies yourself, in-product, against the panel version your baseline was calibrated on. In-product self-service is rolling out — until it lands, reads in this phase are dispatched with us in the loop. New reads land on your desk the same way the baseline did — same evidence currency, same honest report, same lifecycle.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  When an engagement ends, any data already delivered into your own environment stays yours — there is nothing you are required to delete. In-product access ends; the specifics are set in enterprise contracting.
</Note>

## How teams use it

Teams run the rig on a cadence, not once. The common ones:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Quarterly baseline" icon="clock">
    Same mission, same panel version, every quarter — a durable trendline of how agents see your category, comparable read to read.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Competitive watch" icon="radar">
    A standing read on where you land against named rivals in the agent's answer, refreshed on a cadence you set.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Pre / post launch" icon="split">
    A controlled before-and-after on the same instrument: did a site change, a campaign, or a new page move how agents shop you?
  </Card>

  <Card title="Reach audit" icon="circle-half-stroke">
    Which of your surfaces agents can reach and which turn them away — reported per finding, as **reached** or **access-limited**, never as a flat refusal.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What you bring, what we run

| You bring | We run |
| --- | --- |

The whole middle of the rig. |
