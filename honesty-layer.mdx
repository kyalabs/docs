title: "The Honesty Layer"
description: "How a Voice of Agent report stays bound to what actually happened — the structural mechanisms that make an overstated report unreachable rather than merely discouraged."
---

A Voice of Agent report makes claims about how AI shopping agents treated your brand — what they noticed, compared, recommended, and passed over. The value of those claims is exactly the trust you can place in them. A report that overstates is worse than no report: it puts a confident, wrong sentence into a room where someone makes a decision on it.

The Honesty Layer is the part of the pipeline whose entire job is to keep the words in your report from drifting away from what the evidence supports. No human performs a review pass at the end to catch overstatement. Instead, the report is **built** so that the dishonest version is unreachable — not merely discouraged.

This page explains the four mechanisms a technical buyer should understand before trusting the output, and then makes the underlying argument explicit: why this is structural, not editorial. Other pages in this documentation defer here for that argument.

<Note>
  The shortest version: the dishonest output is made unreachable in code, rather than caught after the fact by a reviewer. The trust you place in a report is not a request to trust a model's prose — it is a request to trust arithmetic, a schema contract, and a hold gate, all of which are auditable.
</Note>

## 1 · The headline cannot disagree with the body's numbers

This is the mechanism to understand first, because it is the one most directly load-bearing for an executive who repeats a single sentence in a meeting.

A subtle failure mode in any automated study: the headline and the body of the *same* report quietly disagree. The headline asserts one distribution; the detailed trip-by-trip evidence supports another. Each looks fine in isolation; together they are a fabrication.

Before a report can ship, every counted claim in the headline is reconciled against the audited trip evidence. The auditor and the producer of the headline read from the **same** computed distribution — there is one definition of which trips count, shared by both sides, so the check can never be blind to a contradictory headline. If the numbers the headline asserts do not match the numbers the evidence supports, the report is **held**: it cannot be delivered as clean collateral until the divergence is resolved.

This is a deliberate hard hold, and it is one of only two places in the entire delivery path where a report is stopped rather than shipped-with-disclosure. We reserve hard holds for exactly this: a headline whose distribution contradicts the audited counts. Everything else that could be wrong with a study is disclosed, not held.

<Note>
  The design history is the point. This guard was not added speculatively. It was the response to a live case where a study reached a successful state and presented a clean-looking headline whose distribution the evidence did not support. The detection had already flagged the run — what was missing was *prevention*. The hold is that prevention.
</Note>

We separate two situations on purpose:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="A claim mismatch — stops the report">
    The headline asserts something the audited counts do not support. This hard-holds. It cannot be exported as clean collateral until resolved.
  </Card>
  <Card title="A limited-evidence study — ships, marked">
    A study that simply came back with thin evidence is a different situation. It ships, clearly marked as not research-grade — never silently upgraded into something it isn't.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

The system never launders a limited study into a confident one, and it never holds a study merely for being modest. Those are different defects, and the layer treats them differently.

## 2 · The headline is computed, then narrated — not the other way around

The single most consequential sentence in any report is the headline: the one-line verdict. The obvious risk is that a language model, asked to summarize a study, produces a fluent sentence the underlying trips do not actually support.

We removed that risk by changing the order of operations. Before any narrative is written, code computes the report's thesis directly from the audited evidence:

- which brand each agent landed on, and the tally across all trips;
- the canonical count of how many trips were informative versus planned;
- the verdict class — a clear lead, a contested lead, a genuine split, or no signal;
- a confidence band tied to how many trips actually carried evidence.

**The verdict is a computed object. The language model's job is to narrate that object — it cannot author a different one.** A draft headline that contradicts the computed verdict is replaced with the deterministic one before the report ships.

This inverts the usual failure mode. When a model proposes a conclusion and a checker tries to catch the bad ones, the checker is always one embarrassment behind. Computing the conclusion first turns that around: the thesis exists as a fact before a single sentence is drafted, and the narration is bound to it.

<Note>
  One concrete consequence: if a study has a dominant winner — say one brand led the majority of trips while four rivals each led one or two — the only headline the system can produce names that winner and states the distribution honestly ("leading, not unanimous"). A headline claiming "no single product won," when one clearly did, is not a sentence the pipeline can emit.
</Note>

The one field a model authors freely is the strategic implication — the "so what." Even that is bound to the evidence: it carries its supporting references, and on a validation failure it is omitted rather than fabricated. An absent sentence is preferable to an invented one.

## 3 · The claim boundary travels with the report

Every report carries an explicit statement of what it does and does not claim. A controlled-mission study of real AI shopping surfaces is:

- **not** a market-share measurement;
- **not** a forecast of human demand;
- **not** a public benchmark;
- **not** a ranking of model providers.

We call this the claim boundary, and it is not optional copy a writer might forget. It is a required field on the report's structure, set deterministically when the report is assembled, and rendered onto every delivered surface of the artifact. A report missing its claim boundary fails its own structural contract and does not ship.

This is the discipline made enforceable: counts before rates, evidence references visible, the limits of the read stated on the face of the deliverable — enforced by the schema, not by the diligence of whoever pressed send.

## 4 · A thin study ships marked — it is never dressed up

Agentic studies run against live, changing surfaces. Sometimes a study does not gather enough evidence to support a research-grade read. The dishonest move would be to dress up a thin study as a finished one. The honest move is to say so, in a typed, machine-readable way — and still hand you the artifact, clearly labelled for what it is.

A study that formed but did not clear the evidence bar **ships as a partial success, marked not research-grade**, not silently upgraded into something it isn't. That marking is a first-class, named state on the result — carried in the data, surfaced on the artifact — so a thin-but-usable read is visibly a thin read, never a confident one. The system does not hold a study merely for being modest, and it does not launder a modest study into a polished one. It ships it flagged.

A separate situation is a run that **could not form a study at all**. This is the terminal **failed** state, and it has two causes: the rig failed to operate, or too few of the panel's cells returned results to form a credible read. In either case the run collapses rather than shipping a hollow report; a study that cannot be assembled is not dressed up as one. This is distinct from the thin-but-usable study above, which formed and ships flagged. (Most of the panel's cells must return results for a study to form; the exact floor is qualitative on purpose.) The full buyer-facing state set — **Partial success**, **Failed**, and the held **Delayed** state that stays distinct from both — lives in [Run Lifecycle & Reliability](/run-lifecycle-and-reliability).

<Note>
  **A description of engine behavior.** The not-research-grade marking and the failed state describe how the pipeline behaves today — what the engine does with a thin study and with a run that cannot form one. Read this section as observed pipeline behavior, not as a promise about the outcome of any individual run.
</Note>

### A note on access, because a technical reader will ask

Studies run against surfaces an agent can reach to varying degrees. We describe what an agent could and could not reach in two plain states — **reached** and **access-limited** — rather than asserting an absolute. Where an agent's path to a surface was constrained, the report says so plainly instead of converting a missing observation into a false negative about your brand. A finer characterization of *how* a surface was access-limited rides on the per-finding receipt, underneath that headline state.

This access register is a **methodology discipline**, applied observe-only and graduating toward a stricter check over time. It is **not** a machine-guaranteed property of every sentence. We are deliberate about that distinction: the honest claim is that this is how we discipline the language, not that a validator certifies it on every line today.

## Why this is structural, not editorial

The honest version of all four mechanisms is the same sentence repeated: **the dishonest output is made unreachable in code, rather than caught after the fact by review.**

<Steps>
  <Step title="The headline cannot contradict the body">
    Every counted claim in the headline is reconciled against the audited tally, off one shared distribution. A mismatch holds the report — it cannot ship as clean collateral.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The headline is bound to a computed verdict">
    Winner, distribution, verdict class, and confidence are computed from the evidence before any sentence is drafted. A wrong headline is not a sentence the system can produce.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The claim boundary is a required field">
    What the study does and does not claim is a structural field on every artifact, rendered on every surface — not a disclaimer someone remembers to add.
  </Step>
  <Step title="A thin study ships marked, not dressed up">
    A study that formed but cannot clear the evidence bar ships flagged as not research-grade — visibly a thin read, never silently upgraded. A run that could not form a study at all collapses into the terminal failed state, rather than shipping a hollow report.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Read this as a buyer: the trust you place in a Voice of Agent report is not asking you to trust a model's prose. It is asking you to trust arithmetic, a schema contract, and a hold gate — all of which are auditable, and none of which depend on a human catching a mistake on the way out the door.
