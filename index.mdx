When a shopper asks an AI about your category, an agent answers them — it shops the live web, reads what it can reach, and lands on a recommendation. **Voice of Agent** is a standing research rig that runs that act at scale, on purpose, and brings you the read.

The point of these docs is the opposite of a black box. We show you the whole engine: how a study is composed, how each agent shops, what gets captured, how the report stays bound to the evidence, and what happens when a run can't get a clean read. The hood stays open.

A study runs in five stages. Every claim it produces opens to the receipt behind it.

## The five stages

### ① Question — what you ask

A study begins with a declared question about your category: how the agents real shoppers use treat your brand, and where you land when one of them answers. The question, its denominators, and the classes of claim it can support are written down **before** the run — so results are read against a fixed mission, never against goalposts moved after the fact.

### ② Panel — the instrument

The agents that do the shopping are a **panel**: a weighted sample drawn from GPT, Gemini, Claude, and Perplexity Sonar, locked for the duration of a study and stamped onto every run. Same panel, same question, comparable runs — quarter over quarter. AI models change constantly; the panel is treated as a versioned instrument so a measurement taken in one quarter can be compared to the next, with any change to the instrument recorded as a numbered, visible event rather than silently absorbed.

### ③ Runs — agents shop the live web

Real agents reach the web two different ways, and a study runs both. Some **browse** — they open pages and read them. Some are **search-native** — Perplexity Sonar asks a search-grounded model that answers with citations already attached, and never visits your site directly. These are different acts that see different things, so each agent in the panel retrieves the way the real agent it stands in for retrieves, fixed when the study is set up and never quietly switched to make a run cheaper.

The browse lane regularly meets pages that don't serve automated traffic. We don't flatten that into one word. On any finding, reach is reported honestly as **reached** or **access-limited**, with the finer detail carried on the receipt — so a gap in reach is recorded as what it is, never laundered into a false fact about your site.

### ④ Evidence — per-run receipts

Each run is one agent making one trip, and what it does is written down as it happens — not reconstructed afterward. A run retains its **transcript** (the agent's own turn-by-turn record, redacted of anything personal), its **journey** (the sources it actually touched and where each came from), its **access posture** (reached or access-limited, per attempt), and the plain facts: which model stood in for it, which lane it ran, whether it gathered enough evidence to count.

Every source a run touches becomes one uniform **evidence card**, in the same shape whether it came from a browsed page or a cited source. That shared shape is what a single count across both lanes is built on: browse and search-native cells fold into one source-backed denominator, end-to-end. No claim in the report floats: every finding and every count carries at least one reference back to a card, and that evidence is retrievable.

### ⑤ Report — what lands

The report is a projection of the runs, and it is built so the dishonest version is unreachable rather than merely discouraged. Before any prose is written, the thesis is **computed** from the audited evidence: which brand each agent landed on, how many runs were informative versus planned, the verdict class, and a confidence band tied to how much evidence actually carried. The language model narrates that computed object — it cannot author a different one. A headline that contradicts the underlying counts does not ship.

Counts come before rates. Every claim opens to its evidence. The limits of the read — what a controlled-mission study does and does not claim — travel on the face of the artifact, not as a disclaimer someone might forget.

## Two ways to run it

The same rig serves two postures. They differ in how much of the engine you drive and how much of the raw layer you take.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Pro — self-serve" icon="play">
    **Ask a category question.** You bring the question; the standard panel runs it; the report lands on your desk. On-demand reads in your category, on a standard panel and a set configuration, delivered as the read plus structured data.

    You touch **① Question** and **⑤ Report** — the rig runs the middle.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Custom — open the hood" icon="wrench">
    **Configure the system itself** and take the raw evidence layer. Tune the panel, the model mix, and the persona cohorts; design the missions; work the structured data directly. Custom research scoped to your priorities, with control beyond Pro.

    You open **② Panel**, **③ Runs**, and **④ Evidence** — and take the data.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

The tiers map onto **which stages of the rig you control** — not onto how much of the truth you get. Every study, on either posture, produces the same receipted evidence and the same honest report.
