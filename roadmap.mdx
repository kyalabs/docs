ShoppiPanel is an instrument under active development, and this is the honest version of where it's going. It's shared with design partners on purpose: the order things ship in is a conversation, and your use case is an input to it.

<Note>
  Read the tenses literally. **Live** — in production and receipted. **Rolling out** — built and being turned on; until it lands, that capability runs with us in the loop. **Exploring** — still taking shape, and the most open to your input.
</Note>

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Live today" icon="circle-check">
    - The agent **panel** and its two lanes — browse and search-native — run as one calibrated study.
    - **Receipted reports** — every count opens to its evidence; the headline cannot outrun the trips.
    - **Reach** stated honestly — *reached* vs *access-limited*, never a guess dressed as a fact.
    - The agent's **storefront view** read directly — products, prices, and promotions on the page.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Rolling out" icon="circle-half-stroke">
    - **Self-service dispatch** — run studies yourself, in-product, on your calibrated panel.
    - **Structured outputs** — take the evidence layer as structured data through a stable, API-shaped export.
    - **Deeper surface reads** — richer capture of dynamic, late-loading storefront and category pages.
    - **Reading-a-report guidance** — expanded help for interpreting a study and its receipts.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Exploring" icon="compass">
    - **Portfolio scope** — several brands or categories tracked side by side on one instrument.
    - **Trend views** — the same mission over time, as a competitive or pre/post baseline.
    - **Metered self-serve** — usage-based runs once the instrument is fully in your hands.
    - **Broader coverage** — more shopping surfaces and retail-media placements, as agents reach them.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## This is a conversation

The sequence above is shaped by the partners using ShoppiPanel now. If something in **Rolling out** or **Exploring** is load-bearing for your team — or something you need isn't here at all — that's exactly the feedback that moves it up the list.
