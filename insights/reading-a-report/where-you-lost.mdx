The verdict tells you whether an agent recommended your brand or a competitor. This page is the layer beneath it: when your brand was in the running but lost, the read can show *where* it lost, *to whom*, and *on which proof-point*.

<Note>
  These are **examples of what the [Voice of Agent report](/insights/overview) (the executive read) may surface, not a fixed set of fields.** The read is written by a presenter over the audited evidence, so which diagnostics appear depends on what a given study's evidence supports. Whether you won or lost overall (recommendation and loss itself) is covered on [The headline and the verdict](/insights/reading-a-report/headline-and-verdict); this page is the diagnostic layer beneath it.
</Note>

## The diagnostics the read may surface

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Considered, not chosen" icon="scale-unbalanced">
    Your brand made the agent's consideration set but lost to another option. This one also travels as **structured data** (`non_recommendations`) in the [extract](/under-the-hood/data-outputs), not only in the read. The proof-point gradient beneath it points to what to test next.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Defection" icon="arrow-right-arrow-left">
    Within a trip, the agent moved from your brand to a competitor or alternate as it gathered evidence. The read names where the hand-off happened.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Channel leakage" icon="shuffle">
    The agent routed away from your preferred channel (your DTC site) toward a competing one (a marketplace or retailer): where the buyer's intended path and the agent's chosen path diverged.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Proof-point matrix" icon="table-cells">
    Where you competed and where you lost across the proof-points that decide a category: price, durability, reviews, availability, and the like. The read may lay out the comparison the evidence supports; the full cross-category matrix is fuller-form, roadmap work.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Voice-of-agent quotes" icon="quote-left">
    A real quoted reasoning span from the agent, in its own words, showing why a decision was made: the receipt behind a diagnostic, not a paraphrase.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What this layer is, and is not

- **Examples, not guarantees.** The presenter surfaces a diagnostic when the evidence supports it. A study that produced a clean win, or thin evidence, may surface few of these or none.
- **Counts before rates.** Every diagnostic carries its evidence reference and its denominator. None is a rate dressed as a fact (see [the count ledger](/insights/reading-a-report/count-ledger)).
- **We measure; you interpret.** The read shows where and why the agents landed as they did. What to do about it, the brand decides. No conversion-lift claim, no prescriptive strategy.
