A delivered report opens with a single line. That line is the **verdict**.

The verdict is computed from the count ledger, not authored. The narrative under it explains the "so what." If the counts don't support the headline, the headline doesn't ship.

## The verdict classes

A verdict is one of a fixed set of classes. The class is determined by the metric the study is built to measure and the count ledger behind it.

| Class | What it says | When you see it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Surfaced** | You appear in the agent's answer for the category question, with a frequency above the panel's signal threshold. | A study where the brand reads with consistent agent visibility. |
| **Mixed** | You surface in some agents and personas, not others — the pattern is the finding. | Most real-world reads. The receipts show which agents and personas, and why. |
| **Not surfaced** | You don't appear, or appear below threshold, across the panel. | Categories where the brand isn't yet part of the agent's recommendation set. |
| **Access-limited** | The panel couldn't reach enough of your surfaces to call the question. | A reach issue, not an absence — see the reach findings. |
| **Insufficient** | The denominator is too thin to support any verdict — usually a panel-size or persona-coverage issue. | Rare. Triggers a re-run, not a delivered finding. |

A study can also carry **per-cut verdicts** — by agent, by persona cohort, by surface set — when the question warrants it.

## How confidence is read

Confidence is reported alongside the verdict. It's a function of three things:

- **The denominator** — how many trips actually completed (see [the count ledger](/insights/reading-a-report/count-ledger)).
- **The agreement across the panel** — do the agents agree, or do they split?
- **The access posture** — how much of the relevant surface set was reachable in the runs.

A high-confidence verdict has a full denominator, agreement across the panel, and reached surfaces. A low-confidence verdict will show **why** confidence dropped, on the same page as the verdict itself — not in a footnote.

<Tip>
  Confidence is not a model score. It's a structural property of the run: more reached trips, more agreement, more reachable surface = higher confidence. The math is on the page.
</Tip>

## How to read it in a meeting

A pattern that holds up:

1. **Read the verdict out loud.** One sentence.
2. **State the denominator.** _"X of Y planned trips completed; Z were access-limited."_
3. **Name the cut, if relevant.** _"This holds across browse-native agents; on search-native, the pattern shifts to..."_
4. **Open the receipt if anyone asks why.**

The headline alone is for the executive. The headline plus the denominator is what the analyst defends.
