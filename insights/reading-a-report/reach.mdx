A surface that turns automated traffic away is invisible to the agent shopping your category. The reach findings tell you which surfaces agents reached, and which they couldn't.

Reach is reported **per finding**, never as a flat refusal — because the same surface can be reachable for one agent and blocked for another, and that asymmetry is the finding.

## The two postures

| Posture | What it means |
| --- | --- |
| **Reached** | The agent successfully retrieved the surface — your page loaded, your content was readable, the agent could quote or cite from it. |
| **Access-limited** | The agent attempted the surface and was turned away — a block, a paywall, a bot challenge, a redirect to a login wall, a robots-policy denial, or a soft block (the surface returned but with no usable content). |

Access-limited is not a failure of the rig; it's a finding _of_ the rig. It tells you a surface the agent tried to reach but couldn't — a fixable signal, not a guess.

## What the per-finding receipt adds

A reach finding always opens to a receipt with three things:

1. **Which agent** hit the surface (browse-native? search-native? which model?).
2. **What it tried** — the exact URL, the request, the response.
3. **Why it was access-limited** — the specific block reason (status code, robots clause, soft-block signature, etc.).

This is what separates a reach finding from a guess. You don't get _"agents may have trouble with your site"_ — you get _"GPT-class browse-native agents were blocked from `/products/checkout` by a bot challenge on 12 of 14 attempts; here are the requests."_

## How to act on reach findings

A reach finding is the most operationally actionable output of a study, because it points at a specific surface and a specific block. Common actions:

- **Allowlist** the agent's user-agent or IP range in your bot-management layer.
- **Update robots policy** to permit the specific paths you want agents to read.
- **Move content out of a gate** if it sits behind a login that's blocking agent retrieval.
- **Re-run the study** after the fix and confirm the access posture changed.

The point is to convert an access-limited surface into a reached one, then verify the change on the next read.

<Note>
  Reach reads honestly, both ways. A surface you _want_ blocked from agents (proprietary documentation, customer data) showing up as access-limited is a confirmation, not a problem. The study reports the posture; you decide which surfaces should be which.
</Note>
