Every study returns one artifact: a **report package** — the full shopping trip study, in a form you can read in five minutes or audit line by line.

A five-minute read and a line-by-line audit of the same artifact.

It has three layers, bound in one direction so the headline can never assert a result the counts don't support — [the Honesty Layer](/evidence/honesty-layer) is how that stays true mechanically.

See it on a real study: [a sample ShoppiPanel report](https://www.kyalabs.io/shoppipanel/sample) — the read, the counts behind it, and the receipts, end to end.

## The question a study answers

When a shopper asks an AI about your category — _"what's the best mid-size SUV,"_ _"how should I take payments online,"_ _"which CRM for a 20-person team"_ — an agent shops the live web and lands on a recommendation. That answer is a surface you compete on, and most teams have never seen it.

A study runs that act at scale and brings you the read:

- **Where do you land** when an agent answers your category's question?
- **Which agents** surface you, which pass you over, and which never reach you at all?
- **What did they see** — your live product pages, or only what a search index cited about you?
- **How does that move** quarter over quarter, before and after a launch, against a competitor?

The deliverable is the answer to those questions, with the receipts attached.

## What it is not

Every package states its own limits on its face. A controlled-mission study of real AI shopping surfaces is:

- **not** a market-share measurement
- **not** a forecast of human demand
- **not** a public benchmark or league table
- **not** a ranking of model providers

And it is not a visibility dashboard that scrapes AI answers and charts a mention count. It is a research instrument — an auditable trend, not a score taken on trust:

| An AI-visibility dashboard | A ShoppiPanel study |
| --- | --- |
| A mention count, scraped | A sourced count — every number opens to the trip behind it |
| An asserted percentage | A rate over a stated, metric-eligible denominator |
| One model, one snapshot | A versioned panel of the agents real shoppers use — comparable quarter over quarter |
| "You're not showing up" | Per-finding reach — *reached* or *access-limited*, with the receipt |

<Note>
  This claim boundary is a required field on every report, rendered on every surface of the artifact. [The Honesty Layer](/evidence/honesty-layer) is how that stays true mechanically.
</Note>
