A study studies AI shopping agents against public web surfaces — it does not ingest your consumer or customer data. That single fact defines the posture below. Where a specific control is part of a longer security overview, it's named here and the overview is shareable on request during evaluation.

## Personal data posture

**A study does not require personal data from you to run.**

The inputs we need are a category question, a brand set, and a surface set — none of which are personal data. Persona cohorts are _synthetic shopper profiles_, not real people; they are configured archetypes (e.g., _"SMB IT buyer evaluating CRMs"_), not identities.

| Category | Posture |
| --- | --- |
| **Customer PII** (your customers' personal data) | Not ingested. Not required. The study runs against public web surfaces, not your customer dataset. |
| **Incidental personal data** | A public listing may carry a salesperson name or dealer contact. We treat captured evidence as research material — we do not enrich, re-identify, or build profiles from it. |
| **Employee PII** (your team's data) | Workspace user records only — email, name, role. Standard SaaS-account data. |
| **Persona cohorts** | Synthetic. Not derived from real people. |

## Token scrubbing

Automated scrubbing today is scoped to **secret and credential tokens** — the capture path strips token-shaped strings (token prefixes and `token=`-style query values) from captured text before evidence is persisted.

A content-level, automated _personal-data_ redaction pass over transcripts — detecting and masking emails, phone numbers, names, or government IDs — is **not yet in place**. The real protection is the design itself: the study does not ingest your customer dataset (see above), and a founder reviews the deliverable before handoff.

<Note>
  We do not claim content-verified personal-data redaction today. Automated secret-token scrubbing plus the no-consumer-PII-ingest design is the posture; the content-level personal-data pass is on the roadmap, not live.
</Note>

## Retention

Our stated retention policy:

| Object | Retention window |
| --- | --- |
| **Raw study data** (detailed transcripts, raw captures) | Up to 90 days, then aged out. Deleted on request. |
| **Summarized / derived data** (report package + structured extract) | Up to 365 days for your access and re-reads. Deleted on request. |
| **Logs and queries** | Operational. Deleted on request. |

<Note>
  These are a stated policy, not yet a code-enforced time-to-live. Deletion-on-request is a manual operational commitment by the team today, not an automated lifecycle job. Specific windows are agreed in enterprise contracting and can be shortened for your engagement.
</Note>

## Transport and encryption

| Surface | Standard |
| --- | --- |
| **In transit** | TLS, no downgrade. |
| **At rest** | AES-256. |
| **Cloud-storage handoff** | Delivered into a cloud-storage bucket you designate (S3- or R2-compatible); the exact handoff mechanism is set with you during scoping. |

## Subprocessors

A current subprocessor list is maintained and shareable during evaluation. Material additions are disclosed; the notice terms are set in enterprise contracting.

Categories of subprocessor we use:

- **Cloud infrastructure** (compute, storage, network).
- **Agent providers** (the model vendors whose agents the panel runs).
- **Observability** (logging, metrics, error tracking).

The evidence-**scoring** layer runs on infrastructure we operate. The capture/fetch path — pulling public web content — may use a managed headless-browser provider; where it does, that provider is named in the subprocessor list. Transport (TLS) and at-rest encryption (AES-256) remain provider-backed as described above.

## Incident posture

There is no formally documented incident-response or breach-notification program with contractual timelines today. What exists is operational:

- The team monitors run health and security alerts directly.
- A documented **24-hour kill switch**: on a request to stop touching a named surface, a deploy-config flag halts all traffic to that surface — in-flight and new sessions — within 24 hours, with the refusal recorded in the run's attempt ledger.

A formal incident-response runbook with a defined notification window is a gap we will close as an engagement formalizes; we would commit a notification window in the engagement paper rather than point to a program we do not yet run.

## Security review materials

Available during evaluation, on request:

- **Security overview** (one-pager \+ technical addendum).
- **Subprocessor list** (current).
- **DPA / data addendum** (executed as part of the engagement paper).

<Note>
  We are **not SOC 2 certified** and do not represent otherwise. Our strongest, genuine security position is that the product studies AI shopping agents against public web surfaces and does not ingest your consumer or customer data. We would rather you see the honest posture than discover a gap mid-engagement.
</Note>
