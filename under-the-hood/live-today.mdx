Scope against accurate state. **Live** is shipping in delivered studies right now; **active** is being wired and has a target; **planned** is committed but not yet in active build. Both the shipped and the committed are knowable, so you can plan an engagement without discovering surprises mid-flight.

<Note>
  **Draft — targets illustrative.** Dates target quarter-granularity and represent draft milestones to be confirmed against the current build plan. They move when reality moves. The honest signal here is whether something is committed and being built — not the exact week. Verify dates with your kya labs contact before relying on them for procurement.
</Note>

## What we won't ship

A short list, on the record, of things we've decided against — because _not shipping them_ is part of how the rig stays honest:

- **Real-time partial reads of in-flight studies.** Studies stay immutable until delivered. A half-run isn't a half-truth; it's not yet a finding.
- **Aggregate cross-customer benchmarks.** Your data does not feed someone else's read, ever. Each engagement is its own panel and its own ledger.
- **Model-provider rankings as a deliverable.** The rig measures how agents see your category, not which provider is "best." That's a different study with different ethics.
